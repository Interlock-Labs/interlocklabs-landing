02 How verification works

Deterministic rules. Not another model.

A cartridge encodes expert clinical judgment for one workflow as deterministic rules: range comparisons, string matches, pattern checks. There is no LLM in the verification layer. The same input always produces the same flags, which is what makes the output usable as evidence.

Verification runs before release and again at runtime, and every interaction produces an audit trail with patient data redacted. Interlock measures behavior. It does not certify models, and it does not replace them.

03 Substrate → policy

Coverage doesn't exist because the substrate doesn't exist.

Insurers are not short on appetite. They are short on data. No one can write a policy on a system whose failure modes and failure rates have never been measured. Verification at scale produces exactly that measurement: per-workflow evidence of what clinical AI does, how often it fails, and how severely.

That evidence is the underwriting substrate. Substrate becomes policy. Policy shifts the liability. And liability, shifted, is what makes healthcare AI deployable.
